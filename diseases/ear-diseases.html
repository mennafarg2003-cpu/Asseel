General Overview

Disorders of the outer ear in horses affect the pinna (outer ear) and are commonly caused by parasites, insects, immune reactions, or environmental conditions. Most cases are not limited to the ear alone and may indicate broader skin or systemic disease. Because of the horse’s sensitivity and exposed ear structure, even minor irritation can become painful or complicated if untreated.

Symptoms

✓ Redness and swelling of the ear.

✓ Itching and frequent head shaking.

✓ Small bumps, scabs, or crusts.

✓ Hair loss or rough skin patches.

✓ Pain or sensitivity when touched.

Causes

ℹ️ Tick infestation (including ear ticks).

ℹ️ Mites (psoroptic or sarcoptic mange).

ℹ️ Sarcoids (skin tumors around the ear).

ℹ️ Viral infection (Aural plaques – papillomavirus).

ℹ️ Frostbite (cold and wet weather exposure).

Treatment & Prevention

Immediate Actions:

Clean affected area gently with warm water, Apply veterinarian-prescribed antibiotic or antiparasitic treatment, Remove ticks carefully with forceps Treat pain, infection, or inflammation if present Avoid water entering the ear canal

Ongoing Prevention:

Provide clean water always. Regular feeding schedules, use dewormers periodically, and avoid sudden nutritional changes.
